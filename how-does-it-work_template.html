Getting started is super simple. Hear us out.

1. Sign up using BankID 🤙

First, download the Plonka app. With BankID, you can sign all the necessary forms in a matter of minutes. Since Plonkers are paid based on their performance, you can start or stop working whenever you want - no strings attached. 💯

If you don't love working with us, your account will be terminated automatically after 6 months of inactivity - you don't have to do a thing!

2. Gear up and get Plonking! 🧤

You don't need anything more than a smartphone on a lanyard around your neck and a dataplan to get started - but you may want to wear working gloves and warm clothes. Start up the app and set your sights on the closest clean-up zone. 🚀

Start your clean-up session once you've entered the zone. Your phone will track your location and record a low-resolution video of your session. Dispose of your collected trash in a public bin, end your session, and get paid 50 SEK per filled bag.

3. Payday 😎

By the 25th each month, you'll receive your well-earned balance. Since you are employed by us, we handle all the paperwork, and you don't have to worry about reporting taxes. Can't get any simpler, right? And guess what - you're making a difference for the local environment! 🌳

During your first five sessions, collecting litter in any regular sized plastic bag will do. If you want to continue after this, we will provide you with a free starter kit containing individually numbered Plonka-branded environmentally-friendly plastic bags as well as your choice of working gloves or a picker.

Who pays for all this?

Don't worry - we're not here to rip you off. You won't get rich, but Plonka is a really quick way for you to get a legitimate job - no language skills or previous experience required. Our goal is to get sponsored from a variety of environmentally conscious superstars, such as the local government, companies working on their Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR), and donations from local eco-ninjas.

And why would they want to pay for someone to pick up litter, you ask? Simple - providing social benefits is expensive, and this is a scalable way of providing jobs for pretty much any able-bodied nature-lovers. Additionally, it keeps the local neighborhood clean and safe, both for pets and local wildlife, decreases local poverty, and provides a sense of responsibility for the local environment. So, instead of donating a half a goat (the front or the back, I wonder..?) to a family in a developing, why not help someone closer, while also keeping your neighborhood clean?

The Plonka founders get paid for the time they put in, but this initiative is not meant to be a venture-backed unicorn headed for the stars - we just want to make the world a slightly more fair place. In order to do this, we reserve a maximum of 10% for our own salaries, marketing, and keeping the initiative running - everything else will go to our Plonkers! We aren't completely bonkers, we promise.
